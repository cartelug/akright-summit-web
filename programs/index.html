01The Student Journey

Six steps.
One rising path.

The Summit is not motivational. It is practical because the journey is visible — from book purchase to public recognition, every step is documented.

  1. 01
    Student gets the book.

    Through their school, via the parent circular & pre-launch packet. The book is the on-ramp.

  2. 02
    Student reads & prepares.

    Guided by the framework, supported by teachers, paced by the term schedule.

  3. 03
    School holds internal competition.

    Standard format, supplied scoring sheets, fair eligibility, school coordinator runs.

  4. 04
    Top students advance.

    From school stage to district stage to national stage at the Hub.

  5. 05
    Winners gain recognition.

    Public stage, photographs, certificates, press coverage, prizes — being seen is the point.

  6. 06
    Pitch ideas, connect to leaders.

    Innovation Pitch → Grand Innovation Award. From reader, to thinker, to builder.

02The Programs

Six tracks.
One calendar.

Built to run independently, designed to stack together. A student can enter the Summit through any door — and find the next one waiting.

01

Think Saturday

The anchor program of the Hub. Every Saturday — guided reading, book review groups, mentor talks, and breakout discussions. The training ground where habits are formed and friendships start.

Weekly All Levels · Free
02

Reading Challenge

A termly book-list challenge run inside every partner school. Students earn points for completion, comprehension, and review quality. Top scorers advance to district and national rounds.

Termly 36 Schools · Q3 2026 Start
03

Book Review Stage

Monthly live event. Students present 5-minute book reviews to a panel of mentors and the public audience. Filmed, livestreamed, archived — a real platform for real voice.

Monthly Live · Open Audience
04

Innovation Pitch

Quarterly student-led pitch competition. Take an idea from a book and translate it into a business plan, community solution, or technology concept. Winners pre-qualify for the Grand Innovation Award.

Quarterly Open Entry
05

Mentor Office Hours

Weekly 1-on-1 sessions with founders, executives, professionals, and academics. Booked via the Hub. The fastest way for a student to skip ahead five years of learning.

Weekly By Appointment
06

Grand Innovation Award

The annual flagship competition. The most transformational, scalable innovation proposal — from across all partner schools — wins up to USD 100,000 in development funding, mentorship, and exposure.

Annual USD 100,000 Prize

05Recognition & Rewards

Being seen
changes things.

Every student who advances through the Summit gets photographs, certificates, mentions, and a documented track record. Top performers receive material rewards too.

Public Recognition

Photographs, certificates, on-stage moments, social-media spotlights, and press mentions for advancing students.

Material Rewards

Mobile phones, laptops, educational gadgets — for top performers, sponsored by our partners.

Scholarship Support

School-fee support, scholarship referrals, university preparation help — for students whose journey deserves it.

Mentor Pairings

1-on-1 mentor matching — founders, executives, and academics paired with the most promising students.

Innovation Grants

Seed funding for the most credible innovation proposals — from a few hundred dollars up to the Grand Award.

School Honour Rolls

Partner schools receive trophies, certificates, and assembly recognition for top-performing students.
