01The Origin

A response to a national shift.

Uganda's young people are reading less and scrolling more — and the long-form thinking that builds nations is thinning out with every generation.

Akright Summit is not a literacy programme. It is an intellectual and societal transformation movement designed to produce readers, thinkers, innovators, creators, entrepreneurs, researchers, and transformational leaders — through structured reading, mentorship, public competition, and recognition.

We believe societies are built by thinkers, innovators, and visionaries — and they are developed through disciplined reading, intellectual engagement, mentorship, and the practical application of knowledge in the real world.

The journey is deliberate: Student gets the book → Student reads & prepares → School holds internal competition → Top students advance → Winners gain recognition → Students pitch ideas and connect to leaders.

Strategic Line

"Akright Summit turns reading into competition, competition into confidence, and confidence into enterprise thinking."

Reading Culture Restoration Foundation · Vision Brief

02Vision & Mission

Why we exist.

The Foundation operates on two clear sentences — every program, every partnership, every decision pulls back to these.

Vision

Raise a generation of knowledgeable, innovative, purpose-driven, and solution-oriented young people.

Through the restoration of a strong reading culture across schools, families, and communities — beginning in Uganda, spreading across the continent.

Mission

Create transformational platforms, mentorship systems, reading programmes, and innovation opportunities.

That inspire young people to read deeply, think intelligently, innovate boldly, and apply knowledge practically — for personal, national, and global transformation.
